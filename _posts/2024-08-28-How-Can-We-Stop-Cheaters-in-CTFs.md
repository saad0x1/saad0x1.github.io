Hello there, it's me TheCyberSimon here!
I have spent good amount of time being with CTF Players and communities of these awesome 
Cybersecurity training platforms such as **HackTheBox** & **TryHackMe**. 

I usually see some peoples just pretending to be what think they are "Hackers" but cheating on CTFs and ruining their learning process, recently I have been looking on these peoples behavior,
All they do is Larp about things they just did with writeups or traded the flags of CTFs.
These kinda of so called "hackers" in reality "Larps"  just copy pasting the answers from writeups and making points to show off others that "Oh yea, Look I'm this rank on HTB/THM or Oh yea, My Team scored higher then your one".

At some point I got really tired of these peoples, So I came to a conclusion.
**Why not, I report these cheaters, and also bait them to know if they are cheating in actual**.

Mostly, If I come across a cheater or a flag trader I would ask him simple questions.
For Example, (based on real story). 
I stumbled upon a friends profile on HackTheBox, they got user on **Corporate** machine.
I asked them very simple question about the box, what was the foothold?
They couldn't answer, after couple of mins I got reply from them saying it was **XSS**, I replied with "How did you do the XSS?". again after 2-4 mins later I get reply saying "Oh yea, My friend helped me with it."
I asked how did you figured to bypass **CSP** on the site and got the cookies? again no reply and at the end they told the truth that Yeah, they cheated on the Box.


**Common ways that indicate if someone is cheating on themselves.**

- How did you do this? 
 -> "Oh, I can't remember exactly, I forgot how I did it!"

- Hey, could you help me with this part of this machine/CTF?
 -> "Sorry mate, I'm busy" or "Hmm, let me see my notes" and they never reply.

- They will get stuck on simple problems, like "Oh, I'm having issues with WSL, I can't fix it."
->  You pwned a machine or did a CTF which requires you to do sh** ton of research and googling yet you can't figure out a simple error with WSL.

- They will try to show off and act like they do know everything.


## What I do with these cheaters?
If I stumble upon **sus** activity or someone's sus behavior, I would try to talk and if I see they trying to hide that they cheated, report their profile.
if it's a CTF going, contact the admin as fast you can and provide the proofs.

**I know no one cares if someone cheats or not?!**
But I do if someone is interested in learning about things but ends up using writeups or cheating.
It hurts to see when someone just ruining their learning, it's more of a personal experience.
when I started I went through the same thing, but Now I do realize my mistake and I don't want to cause others from same thing.

One of the things is Platforms **TOS** like you can't make writeup of active content on HTB or can't publish writeup before 72-hrs on THM, such platforms have their own rules and TOS.
But some people still make, so point of this mentioning that these things encourage newbies
to do Active content on HTB to get points and show off to others that "Hey I did a hard machine on HTB, purely an insane machine marked as hard."

## How do we stop it?

- Flags should be dynamic!
- Cheaters should be reported as fast as you can, reach out to the Admins.
- If it's a platform, for example: If it's HTB, Send the writeup of active content or cheater's profile directly to HTB-Staff.
